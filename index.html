What this tab computes. Given arm-level survival curves you set, it (1) checks them against the announced blinded event counts, (2) estimates the hazard ratio and its statistical significance, and (3) runs a Bayesian Monte-Carlo posterior over the final HR.

1 · Survival & events

BAT arm = Weibull (shape k, k=1 → exponential) + a long-survivor "cure" fraction; GPS arm = mixture-cure (plateau c + uncured exponential) with an optional onset delay; optional post-enrollment transplant tail (ITT) added to both. Expected deaths by calendar month T = Σ over the enrollment S-curve of (1 − S(T − enroll)), ×(1 − censoring/2).

2 · Hazard ratio & significance

HR via the Pike/O–E estimator. Significance via the proper stratified log-rank score Z = (Σ w·(O−E))/√(Σ w²·V) · √(strat-efficiency) computed from the curves — so a late-loaded effect is correctly penalized (unweighted w=1; Fleming-Harrington w=1−S_pool). Win = Z ≥ 2.01 (the O'Brien-Fleming final boundary). Schoenfeld: 90% power at HR 0.636 needs ~205 events, so 0.636 at 80 events is the ~50%-power bar.

3 · Monte-Carlo posterior

Sample priors (slider = center, 1σ band = spread) → weight each draw by the Poisson likelihood of the observed event increments (60, +12, +6, still <80) → for a binding efficacy interim, soft-condition on "no early stop" (weight = P(continue); win = conditional power via the Brownian-motion correlation √(t₁/t₂)) — power-neutral, banks early wins. P(win) = Σ(w·p_win)/Σ(w).
Poke holes: the arm split is unidentifiable (blinded); priors do heavy lifting; the log-rank z is an expectation, not a patient-level resimulation; stratification/futility/test-weighting are undisclosed (adjustable). Full citations in References below.

Monte Carlo — final-HR distribution

Samples the priors — each slider = the prior CENTER, its blue 1σ band = the spread — and weights each draw by a Poisson likelihood of the observed event increments (60 by m46, +12, +6, and still <80), so every scenario is anchored in proportion to how well it explains the exact numbers, with counting noise modeled — not accepted/rejected by an arbitrary window. "Win" is now a proper stratified log-rank significance test (probabilistic, NPH-aware — a late-loaded effect is penalized), and the interim is handled correctly: with "binding efficacy" checked, worlds are soft-weighted by P(continuing without an early stop) and scored by their conditional power — which banks early wins and is power-neutral (not the crude hard cliff). A mild futility pass and a stratification efficiency haircut are applied. Move any slider to shift a prior, then re-run. Approximate-Bayesian posterior — only as good as the priors. Tip: click "Set neutral (literature) priors" for the balanced base case (~65–68% win); the fitting presets (Base/Bull/etc.) center the priors on a strong-effect scenario, so their MC will read more optimistic.

The bet in one line: the announced event pace implies pooled survival well above historical CR2, so a real benefit is more likely than not — but not established: a no-effect world with a ~25–30% shared long-survivor tail also fits (HR=1.0). The magnitude (HR) and the arm split are unidentifiable from outside the blind; a large undisclosed BAT tail, heavy differential censoring (event model ignores censoring, which biases the implied benefit optimistic), or events never reaching 80 (power) are the ways a "win" shrinks or evaporates. Threshold to win (0.636) is modest.

Statistical methods (primary sources)

  1. Log-rank / score test (our U/√V significance): Mantel 1966; Peto & Peto, JRSS A 1972. Cox PH model: Cox, JRSS B 1972.
  2. Events-for-power (Schoenfeld): D = (zα+zβ)² / [r(1−r)(ln HR)²]. Schoenfeld, Biometrika 1981 & Biometrics 1983. calculator
  3. α-spending & group-sequential boundaries: O'Brien & Fleming, Biometrics 1979; Lan & DeMets, Biometrika 1983.
  4. Conditional power & sequential-statistic correlation √(t₁/t₂) (Brownian-motion score process): Jennison & Turnbull, Group Sequential Methods with Applications to Clinical Trials (2000); Proschan & Hunsberger, Biometrics 1995.
  5. RMST (NPH-robust effect measure): Uno et al., J Clin Oncol 2014. RMST vs HR
  6. Weighted log-rank / late-effect penalty (Fleming-Harrington): Fleming & Harrington, Counting Processes and Survival Analysis (1991); for cancer-vaccine delayed effects: Hasegawa, Pharm Stat 2014. PubMed
  7. Mixture-cure / plateau models: Boag, JRSS B 1949; Berkson & Gage, JASA 1952.
  8. Lead-time / left-truncation (immortal-time) bias (the CR2→randomization window): Suissa, Am J Epidemiol 2008; Suissa, Pharmacoepidemiol Drug Saf 2007. AJE 2008
  9. Approximate Bayesian Computation (our likelihood-weighting of the event counts): Beaumont, Zhang & Balding, Genetics 2002; Sisson, Fan & Beaumont, Handbook of ABC (2018).

Different trial, different math. SLS-009 (tambiciclib; a selective CDK9 inhibitor, licensed from GenFleet) is in a single-arm Phase 2 with azacitidine + venetoclax — no randomized control, so efficacy is judged against a historical benchmark.

Effect measures

Both arms modeled as exponential OS. Fold-improvement = arm median ÷ benchmark median. Under exponential survival the HR-equivalent = benchmark median ÷ arm median (constant hazard ratio). Months gained = difference of medians.

Regulatory path (heuristic)

Accelerated approval in r/r AML rests on ORR + a clear OS signal vs a dismal benchmark. The "approvability" bar rises with ORR and OS fold-improvement — a judgment aid, not a validated model.

Frontline (the bigger prize)

Newly-diagnosed control is Aza/Ven (VIALE-A mOS 14.7m); a Phase 3 there would use a stratified log-rank like REGAL. We show median-OS uplift only.
Poke holes: single-arm-vs-historical is confounded by selection; ORR ≠ OS; the ~2.5-mo benchmark and 8.9-mo arm are small Phase-2 cohorts; frontline figures are projections.

Peak-sales × multiple, with a survival-driven prevalence pool.

The key dynamic (population grows if patients survive)

At steady state, patients on therapy = annual new starts × penetration × average years on therapy. Longer survival ⇒ more years on drug ⇒ a larger prevalent pool. Peak annual revenue = new-starts/yr × penetration × avg-years × annual price.

Valuation

Enterprise value = total peak sales × multiple + risk-adjusted platform value. Oncology assets historically trade at ~4–8× peak sales (market convention, not a fixed fact). Per share = EV ÷ fully-diluted shares.

WT1 platform

WT1 was ranked the #1 cancer antigen by an NCI working group (Cheever 2009) and is over-expressed across 20+ tumor types (AML, MDS, mesothelioma, ovarian, glioblastoma…). The platform value is a single risk-adjusted lump you set — deliberately crude.
Poke holes: penetration, price, duration and the multiple are all assumptions; the addressable pools are estimates; this ignores time-value/discounting, competition, and dilution beyond the share count; it is a back-of-envelope, not a DCF.
